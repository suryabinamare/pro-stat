Hypothesis Testing: Single Population Mean (\(\sigma\))

We apply sample data and statistical tools to test whether the mean of a single population is equal to a known or hypothesized value.

When population standard deviation, \(\sigma\) known:
  1. Two-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu \neq \mu_0\)
  2. Left-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu < \mu_0\)
  3. Right-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu > \mu_0\)

We need these values:

  • Sample mean: \(\bar{X}\)
  • Population mean: \(\mu_0\)
  • Sample size: \(n\)
  • Pop Std: \(\sigma\)
  • Significance Level: \(\alpha\)

We find the following values:

  • Test Statistic, \(Z\) = \( \frac{\bar{X} - \mu_0}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} \)
  • P-value = \( P(Z < z) \) for left-tailed, \( P(Z > z) \) for right-tailed, and \( 2 \cdot P(Z > |z|) \) for two-tailed tests.
  • Critical Values:
    • \(\pm z_{\alpha/2}\) for two-tailed test
    • \( z_{\alpha} \) for right-tailed test
    • -\( z_{\alpha} \) for left-tailed test
  • Confidence Interval for population mean, \(\mu_0\):
    • CI =\( \left(\bar{X} - z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{X} + z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \) for two-tailed test
    • CI = \( \left(\bar{X} - z_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{X} + z_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \) for right-tailed test
    • CI = \( \left(\bar{X} - z_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{X} + z_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \) for left-tailed test

Conclusion:

  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if Test Statistic, \(z_t\) is in the non-rejection interval for test statistic. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if P-Value is bigger than Alpha. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if the confidence interval contains the null hypothesis value. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
