Hypothesis Testing: Single Population Mean (unknown variance)

We apply sample data and statistical tools to test whether the mean of a single population is equal to a known or hypothesized value.

When population standard deviation, \(\sigma\) unknown:
  1. Two-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu \neq \mu_0\)
  2. Left-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu < \mu_0\)
  3. Right-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu = \mu_0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu > \mu_0\)

We need these values:

  • Sample mean: \(\bar{X}\)
  • Population mean: \(\mu_0\)
  • Sample size: \(n\)
  • Sample std: \(s\)
  • Significance level: \(\alpha\)
  • Degrees of freedom: \(df = n - 1\)

We find the following values:

  • Test Statistic, \(T\) = \( \frac{\bar{X} - \mu_0}{s / \sqrt{n}} \)
  • P-value = \( P(T < t) \) for left-tailed, \( P(T > t) \) for right-tailed, and \( 2 \cdot P(T > |t|) \) for two-tailed tests.
  • Critical Values:
    • \(\pm t_{\alpha/2}\) for two-tailed test
    • \( t_{\alpha} \) for right-tailed test
    • -\( t_{\alpha} \) for left-tailed test
  • Confidence Interval for population mean, \(\mu_0\):
    • CI =\( \left(\bar{X} - t_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{X} + t_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \) for two-tailed test.
    • CI = \( (\bar{X} - t_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}, none) \) for right-tailed test.
    • CI = \(\left(none, \bar{X} + t_{\alpha} \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \) for left-tailed test.

Conclusion:

  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if Test Statistic, \(t\) is in the non-rejection interval for test statistic. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if P-Value is bigger than Alpha. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if the confidence interval contains the null hypothesis value. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
